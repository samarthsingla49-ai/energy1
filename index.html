Project Details & Concepts

What is Panel Angle (Tilt)?

The Panel Angle or Tilt Angle is the most important setting for a solar heater. Think of it as aiming a camera: to get the best picture, you have to point it directly at the target. Since Patiala is in the northern hemisphere, the sun stays toward the south. In the winter, the sun is very low in the sky, so we need to tilt the panel up to a steeper angle (about 45°) to catch the light directly. If the panel is too flat, the sun’s rays just bounce off the glass instead of heating the water inside. Proper tilting can increase the hot water produced by over 20% without adding any extra equipment.

Best Practice: Winter = 45° | Summer = 15°

Solar Heat (GHI) in Patiala

Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) is just a fancy way of measuring how much total sun energy hits the ground in Patiala. It includes direct sunbeams and the light that scatters through clouds. In Patiala, the sun is very strong in the summer, giving us lots of heat. However, in the winter, Patiala often gets very foggy or smoky due to crop residue burning. This creates a "haze" that blocks the sun. Our model accounts for this by showing a "Haze Overlay" in winter. We recommend using Vacuum Tube Collectors because they work much better than flat panels at catching heat even when it's foggy or hazy outside.

Note: Winter fog reduces heat by nearly 50% in Punjab.

Water Hotness (Outlet Temperature)

The Outlet Temperature is simply the temperature of the water when it is ready for students to use in the hostel showers. Our goal is to keep this between 55°C and 65°C. This temperature is calculated based on how much sun we catch and how well the tank is insulated. If we don't use good insulation (like PUF foam), the water will lose its heat overnight, and it will be cold by the time students wake up for 8 AM classes. In our simulation, the "Water Hotness" gauge shows you exactly how hot the water will be based on the number of students using it and the current angle of the sun.

Target: Constant 60°C supply for student hygiene.
